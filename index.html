Common questions

Everything you want to know

What exactly is the 20-20-20 rule?

Every 20 minutes, look at something at least 20 feet away for 20 seconds. This simple habit relaxes the ciliary muscle inside your eye, which is constantly contracting when you focus on a nearby screen. It's the most widely recommended method to reduce digital eye strain and prevent computer vision syndrome.

Does it slow down my browser or drain my battery?

No — EyeRest uses Chrome's native chrome.alarms API, which is the same low-power mechanism Chrome itself uses for background tasks. There is no active polling, no background scripts running constantly, and no measurable impact on performance or battery life.

Does EyeRest collect any data or need an account?

Absolutely not. EyeRest has no backend, no servers, and no analytics. Your preferences and usage data never leave your device. There is no sign-up, no email required, and nothing to log in to — ever.

Can I adjust the reminder interval or turn it off temporarily?

Yes. You can change the break interval, snooze reminders when you're in a meeting or a focus sprint, or pause the extension entirely with one click. It's designed to work around your schedule, not the other way around.

I already use blue light glasses. Do I still need this?

Blue light glasses help with glare and light wavelength, but they don't address muscle fatigue — which is the main cause of eye strain for most screen workers. The 20-20-20 rule targets the focusing muscles directly. They work best together, not as alternatives.

Is the source code available to review?

Yes. EyeRest is open source and the full code is available on GitHub. You can inspect every line before installing, which is exactly how a privacy-first tool should work. No hidden scripts, no obfuscated logic.
